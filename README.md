# Do you wear your helmet properly?

## Background

India has one of the highest numbers of road traffic accidents globally, making road safety a critical public health concern. According to MoRTH and NCRB data, we recorded around 4.47L road accidents and 1.3L fatalities on 2022-23. 

Among those, two-wheelers were the most vulnerable group. They account for the largest share of fatalities in road accidents:

 - Approximately 45–46% of total road accident deaths involve two-wheeler users.

 - In absolute terms, that’s ~79,000+ deaths annually due to two-wheeler crashes alone. 

This high vulnerability is primarily due to:

 - Lack of passive safety infrastructure compared to car.

 - Frequent non-compliance with helmet usage.

Regionally, states like Tamil Nadu and Uttar Pradesh report some of the highest numbers of two-wheeler accident fatalities, indicating both high vehicle density and road safety challenges.

## Problem Statement

To study the influence of helmet design and fit parameters using a parametric finite element model, and to identify an optimal configuration for enhanced safety under different impact scenario.

This project was done as a part of ED6002 *Optimization in Engineering Design* course, taught at IIT Madras.
